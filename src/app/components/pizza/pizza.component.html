Understanding Fractions

Fractions are like pieces of a pizza or cake! They help us show how something is split into equal parts.

The Top Number (numerator) tells you how many parts you have, and the Bottom Number (denominator) tells you how many equal parts the whole is divided into.

For example, the fraction 1/4 means:

You have 1 piece out of 4 equal pieces of something, like a pizza that's divided into 4 slices, and you have one slice!

Fractions are used in dialy life! For Example :

1) Cutting a pizza into equal slices

2) Splitting a bar of chocolate amoung friends

3) Measuring Ingredients while cooking (e.g., 1/2) cup of sugar.

Fractions help us divide and share things equally

Different Types of Fractions

Proper Fraction: The Numerator is Smaller than the Denominator (e.g., 3/4)

ImProper Fraction: The Numerator is Larger than the Denominator (e.g., 5/4)

Mixed Fraction: A Whole number combined with a fraction (e.g., 2 1/2 means..Having 2 full pizzas and then half of a third pizza.)

Comparing Fractions

Find a common denominator or use cross-multiplication

Example: Which is bigger, 2/3 or 3/4 ?
Convert them to have the same denominator or cross-multiply: 2 x 4 = 8 and 3 x 3 = 9. Since 9 > 8, 3/4 is greater than 2/3!
